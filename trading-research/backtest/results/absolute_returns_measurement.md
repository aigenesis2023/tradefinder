## Break-even math (10d horizon, +8%/-6% framework, 60bps round-trip costs)

- Actual 10d win rate (any positive return): **52.7%**
- Actual 10d mean return: **1.84%**
- Break-even win rate needed for +8%/-6% framework after 60bps costs: **47.1%**
- Naive expectancy if every win = +8% and every loss = -6%, after costs: **0.78%** per trade

Caveats: actual fills rarely hit exactly +8% or -6%. Above is a sanity check, not an estimate.
The 'mean return' line is the actual measurement and is more reliable.

## Reading guide

- **Mean vs Median**: if mean >> median, distribution is right-skewed (driven by big winners). Concerning for retail because you cannot rely on the mean.
- **Win rate**: % of trades where absolute return > 0. The number that matters for cash P&L.
- **Trimmed mean vs Full mean**: if removing 5 best + 5 worst trades collapses the mean, the edge is concentrated in outliers, not a robust effect.
- **p10**: 10th percentile — the typical bad trade. If p10 is very negative, a single losing streak does heavy damage.
- **near_52w_low slice**: if distress sub-cohort underperforms, the engine should add a distress/conviction tag in v2.
- **Cluster size slice**: if 5+ (elite) sample is too small (n<10), it cannot support a sizing multiplier.
- **Year slice**: if results are concentrated in 1-2 years, out-of-sample stability is suspect.
