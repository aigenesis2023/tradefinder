## HYPOTHESIS 1

HYPOTHESIS NAME: Dealer Gamma Imbalance and Next-Day Strike Magnetism
SOURCE AGENT: Microstructure Mechanic
MECHANISM: Options market makers delta-hedge their gamma exposure, and the aggregate direction of that hedging is predictable from end-of-day open interest data published by CBOE/OPRA. When dealers are net short gamma (measured as the dollar gamma of all outstanding options on a stock, weighted by direction and proximity), their hedging is procyclical — they buy as the stock rises and sell as it falls, amplifying intraday trends and causing "acceleration zones." More specifically, at specific strike prices where gamma flips sign (from net short above the strike to net long below, or vice versa), the hedging flow changes from trend-amplifying to mean-reverting. These "gamma flip" strikes act as price magnets: stocks approaching them while dealers are net short gamma will (a) accelerate through the strike due to short-gamma hedging, then (b) reverse within the session as the gamma sign flips and hedging becomes mean-reverting. By calculating the aggregate dealer gamma profile at market close using CBOE open interest data, a trader can identify the precise strike levels where this signature pattern is most likely to manifest the next trading day — and place limit orders the night before to capture it. The edge comes from pre-positioning at gamma flip levels before the session opens, while most market participants only react to price during the session.

LLM ADVANTAGE: Two LLM-specific abilities. First, the LLM reads overnight and pre-market news (earnings releases, guidance updates, analyst initiations, macro events) and classifies whether any fundamental catalyst exists that could trigger a directional breakout through the gamma strike — cases where the mechanical gamma signature would be overwhelmed by information flow. A pure gamma model cannot distinguish "SPY will pin at 450 because dealers are long gamma there" from "SPY will blast through 450 because NVDA just pre-announced." The LLM filters out fundamentally-driven days, leaving only the mechanically-driven ones in the signal. Second, the LLM reads the option chain data descriptions (from CBOE quote feeds, which include unusual-open-interest flags and trade condition codes readable as natural language) to identify whether the open interest at key strikes is customer-driven (meaningful gamma) or market-maker-to-market-maker (spread trades with no directional hedging implications). Rules-based systems treat all open interest equally; the LLM distinguishes hedging-relevant OI from noise.

WHY UNDERWEIGHTED: Gamma hedging is a well-known phenomenon among institutional options desks and market makers themselves. However, the specific combination of (a) retail-accessible end-of-day OI data, (b) LLM-based news catalyst filtering, (c) strike-level gamma flip identification, and (d) next-day pre-positioning via limit orders is under-exploited because: (1) Institutions trading off gamma do so intraday with real-time data feeds costing $10K+/year — they do not use end-of-day data. (2) Retail traders generally lack the computational tools to calculate aggregate gamma profiles from raw OI data. (3) The LLM's news-filtering step — reading overnight headlines and determining whether the news is sufficient to overwhelm the gamma structure — is genuinely new; it bridges structured quantitative data with unstructured qualitative context in a way that neither pure quant models nor human discretionary traders can easily replicate at scale across hundreds of stocks.

HOLDING PERIOD: Intraday (next-day session). Positions set via limit orders placed after market close, executed during the following regular trading hours, and exited by that day's close.
DATA REQUIREMENT:
- CBOE end-of-day open interest and option chain data (free via CBOE delayed quotes API or Yahoo Finance options chains; CBOE also provides a free All Access API with registration for non-commercial use — end-of-day data covers all listed equity options).
- Stock price and volume data (Yahoo Finance, free).
- News headlines and full-text articles from the prior market close to the next open (SEC EDGAR for filings released after hours, Yahoo Finance news, PR Newswire, BusinessWire — all free).
- Option trade condition codes and open interest flags from CBOE quote data (available in the options chain display, free).
- Historical data covering at minimum 2019-2024 for backtesting (5 years, available via Yahoo Finance).

PROCESSING APPROACH:
1. LLM extracts structured open interest data from CBOE JSON/CSV output — specifically strike, expiration, call/put, open interest, and trade condition flags — then feeds it to a deterministic gamma calculation script that computes dollar gamma per strike (gamma * underlying price * open interest * 100 shares) and aggregates into the net dealer gamma curve. This computational step is deterministic, not LLM-based.
2. LLM reads the overnight news corpus (filings released 4pm-9:30am ET, pre-market headlines, after-hours earnings calls) and classifies each stock into one of three categories: FUNDAMENTAL_CATALYST (material news likely to produce a directional move exceeding 2x normal daily range), AMBIGUOUS (minor news), or NO_CATALYST (no meaningful news). This is the core LLM task — holistic semantic classification.
3. LLM reads trade condition codes and open interest annotations from the options chain for the 3 strikes nearest the current price, classifying each as CUSTOMER_DRIVEN (OI at this strike likely represents genuine hedging demand), DEALER_TO_DEALER (spread/butterfly activity, low hedging relevance), or INDETERMINATE. This filters gamma calculations to include only hedging-relevant OI.
4. Signal: Enter when (a) stock is in top quintile of dealer short gamma magnitude relative to its 20-day average daily volume, (b) LLM classifies as NO_CATALYST, (c) the nearest gamma-flip strike is within 1.5% of prior close. Place buy limit orders 5bps above the gamma-flip strike (long entry below strike where gamma flips from short to long) and sell limit orders 5bps below the gamma-flip strike (short entry above strike). Exit at the close or at a 1% stop loss from entry.

FALSIFIABLE PREDICTION: If this edge is real, a basket of stocks meeting all three entry conditions (high short gamma, no catalyst, gamma flip within 1.5%) should exhibit a next-day intraday reversal from the gamma-flip strike at a rate greater than 58% (measured as: price touches within 10bps of the strike, then reverses by at least 0.5% within the same session). If fake, the reversal rate should be indistinguishable from random — between 47% and 53%, consistent with the null hypothesis that price paths near arbitrary levels show no directional bias. Secondary test: the mean intraday range for signal stocks should be narrower than the mean intraday range for a matched control group of stocks with similar volatility but no gamma concentration, since the gamma pinning effect, if real, should reduce realized volatility around the strike.

MINIMUM EFFECT SIZE: Per-trade expected value must exceed 25 basis points after estimated costs (commission = 0bps with zero-commission broker; spread = 3-5bps for liquid single-stock options underlying; slippage on limit orders = 2-3bps; total round-trip cost ~8-12bps). Annualized: assuming 50-100 signal days per year across a watchlist of 200 liquid equities with listed options, net alpha > 8% annualized after costs, with a Sharpe ratio > 0.5. Below 25bps per trade, the strategy is economically indistinguishable from noise for a $100K account (profit per trade = $250, which at 1-2 trades per signal day barely covers the time cost of end-of-day analysis).

OUT-OF-SAMPLE PLAN:
1. Train gamma calculation parameters (lookback window for ADTV, gamma magnitude thresholds) on 2019-2021 data.
2. Validate LLM catalyst classification prompts on 2022 data — refine classification criteria without seeing 2023-2024 results.
3. Test full signal on 2023-2024 data with NO parameter changes — pure out-of-sample.
4. Cross-validate across market regimes: test separately on VIX < 20 (low vol), VIX 20-30 (normal vol), VIX > 30 (high vol) periods.
5. Cross-validate across stock liquidity tiers: top 100 by options volume vs. stocks ranked 101-500.

SELF-ASSESSED CONFIDENCE: Medium
BIGGEST WEAKNESS: Gamma calculations from end-of-day open interest are inherently stale by the next morning's open — new positions opened, existing positions closed, and intraday hedging already partly digested the prior day's gamma imbalance into closing prices. The edge relies on the gamma profile having multi-day persistence (options open interest does not turn over completely in one session), but if the market has already front-run the gamma flip levels in the overnight futures session, the limit orders will never fill at advantageous prices. Additionally, the LLM news classification task is high-stakes: a single misclassified earnings pre-announcement (classifying a material catalyst as NO_CATALYST) could produce a 5%+ adverse move through the limit order, wiping out weeks of small gains. The false-negative rate on catalyst classification must be extremely low for the strategy to survive.

## HYPOTHESIS 2

HYPOTHESIS NAME: Pre-Earnings Abnormal Short Flow as a Predictor of Post-Earnings Mechanical Covering
SOURCE AGENT: Microstructure Mechanic
MECHANISM: Daily short sale volume reported by FINRA (Rule 4560) reveals the timing pattern of short positioning ahead of scheduled earnings announcements. When short volume as a percentage of total volume (SV/Total) spikes into the top quintile of its trailing-90-day distribution during the 5 trading days immediately before an earnings release, it indicates aggressive short positioning for a bearish earnings outcome. If the earnings call transcript subsequently reveals a neutral or positive assessment — measured not by the earnings beat/miss versus analyst estimates (which is noisy and gamed), but by the qualitative content of management's forward guidance, tone, and response to analyst questions — then the pre-earnings shorts are "trapped": they positioned for a negative catalyst that did not materialize. These shorts must cover mechanically for three reasons: (1) risk management mandates that close positions after the catalyst passes regardless of conviction, (2) the borrow cost on hard-to-borrow names creates negative carry that accelerates covering pressure, and (3) the initial post-earnings price rise triggers stop-losses on remaining shorts, creating a cascading covering feedback loop. The predictable upward pressure from mechanical covering produces excess returns in the 1-5 trading days following the earnings call, concentrated in stocks with elevated borrow fees and high pre-earnings short flow.

LLM ADVANTAGE: The LLM's specific advantage is holistic earnings call classification that transcends mechanical beat/miss metrics. The LLM reads the full earnings call transcript (management prepared remarks, Q&A with analysts, tone, hedging language, guidance specificity) and classifies whether the call content is LIKELY_TO_SQUEEZE (management tone neutral-to-positive about forward outlook, short thesis catalysts were not validated), LIKELY_TO_CONFIRM_SHORTS (management explicitly validated bear concerns — guidance cut, demand deterioration, competitive loss), or AMBIGUOUS (mixed signals). A traditional quant model would use earnings surprise vs. estimates, which is gamed by guidance management and misses the qualitative forward-looking content that determines whether shorts will cover. Rules-based NLP (sentiment lexicons, bag-of-words) cannot capture the nuanced distinction between "we beat this quarter but guided down sharply" (shorts are right, cover signal is fake) and "we missed this quarter but the forward outlook is dramatically improving" (shorts are wrong, true squeeze signal). The LLM synthesizes the entire transcript holistically, including detecting when management is obfuscating, using inconsistent hedging language, or revealing strength/weakness through Analyst Q&A tone shifts rather than explicit statements.

WHY UNDERWEIGHTED: (1) FINRA short volume data is public but noisy — it includes market-maker shorting for liquidity provision, not just directional shorting. Parsing the signal from noise requires filtering by context (is this a hard-to-borrow name? is the shorting concentrated near earnings or just normal liquidity provision?). The LLM's transcript analysis provides the missing context filter. (2) Institutional short-sellers typically exit based on their own fundamental analysis, not mechanical pressure — but retail shorts and smaller hedge funds face risk-management constraints that force mechanical covering. This creates a retail-scale edge that institutions cannot capture at scale because their own covering would move the price. (3) The combination of FINRA short volume data, securities lending data (borrow fee, shares available), and LLM transcript analysis is a three-source synthesis that is too labor-intensive for discretionary traders and too unstructured for traditional quant systems.

HOLDING PERIOD: 1-5 days post-earnings-call. Enter at the close of the trading day after the earnings call is published (allowing one full session for the call content to be processed by the LLM and the trade to be placed). Exit after 5 trading days or at a trailing stop of 3% from entry, whichever comes first.

DATA REQUIREMENT:
- FINRA daily short sale volume by ticker (free via FINRA OTC Transparency / ADF data; for listed equities traded on-exchange, the exchange-reported short volume is available from NYSE/Nasdaq daily TAQ summaries, but FINRA's off-exchange short volume data is also available free and covers a significant portion of retail short flow).
- Bi-monthly short interest (FINRA, free — reported every 2 weeks with ~10-day lag) and daily securities lending data: borrow fee rate, shares available to borrow (Interactive Brokers provides this free to account holders via TWS API; iborrowdesk.com aggregates it free with daily updates for all IB-available names, albeit with modest lag for non-subscribers).
- Earnings call transcripts (Motley Fool / Seeking Alpha publish free transcripts within hours of calls; Yahoo Finance aggregates them).
- Stock price and volume data (Yahoo Finance, free).
- Historical data: 2019-2024.

PROCESSING APPROACH:
1. Deterministic pre-filter: For each stock with a scheduled earnings announcement, compute the 5-day pre-earnings average short volume as a percentage of total volume (SV%). Flag stocks where SV% is in the top quintile of the ticker's own trailing-90-day distribution (to control for stocks that normally have high short volume due to options market-making).
2. Simultaneously, pull borrow fee rate and shares-available-to-borrow data from IB or iborrowdesk.com. Flag stocks where borrow fee exceeds 10% annualized (indicating genuine short demand, not just market-maker activity) and available shares are below 20% of total shares short.
3. LLM reads the full earnings call transcript (released within hours of the call ending) and produces a structured classification: SQUEEZE_SETUP, SHORTS_CONFIRMED, or NO_EDGE. The classification criteria: (a) Did management's forward guidance indicate year-over-year growth, stability, or contraction? (b) Did the Q&A reveal analyst skepticism that management credibly rebutted, or did management struggle to answer? (c) Did management use increasing or decreasing hedging language (hedge-word density analysis: compare "we believe" / "subject to" / "uncertain" frequency vs. prior quarter call)? (d) Did management disclose any information that specifically addresses the likely short thesis (e.g., if short thesis is "losing market share to competitor X," does the call contain any mention of competitive dynamics, win/loss rates, or market share)?
4. Signal: Enter long at the close on the day the transcript is published when (a) pre-earnings SV% is in the top quintile of its 90-day range, (b) borrow fee > 10% annualized, (c) LLM classifies as SQUEEZE_SETUP. Exit after 5 trading days or at 3% trailing stop-loss.

FALSIFIABLE PREDICTION: If this edge is real, stocks meeting all three criteria (high pre-earnings SV%, elevated borrow fee, LLM SQUEEZE_SETUP classification) should show a mean 5-day post-earnings excess return of at least 2.5% above the sector-matched control group (stocks with similar market cap, same sector, same earnings week, but normal pre-earnings SV%). The hit rate (positive 5-day excess return) should exceed 60%. If fake, the mean excess return should be within the range -0.5% to +0.5% (indistinguishable from zero), and the hit rate should be between 47-53%. Additional falsification: if the signal is real, the excess return should be positively correlated with borrow fee (higher borrow fee = more acute short pressure = larger covering bounce). If fake, there should be no monotonic relationship between borrow fee and post-earnings return.

MINIMUM EFFECT SIZE: Mean per-trade excess return of at least 2.0% after borrow costs for hard-to-borrow names (borrow fee prorated for 5-day hold). For a $100K account with 10% position sizing ($10K per trade) and ~40-60 qualifying signals per year across the US equity universe, this implies annualized excess returns of 80-120% of account equity from the strategy alone, which is unrealistic at scale — so the real test is whether the signal maintains > 1.5% per trade when position sizes are constrained to 3% of the stock's average daily volume (the actual retail capacity constraint). If capacity-constrained returns fall below 0.5% per trade, the signal is economically dominated by a buy-and-hold small-cap index.

OUT-OF-SAMPLE PLAN:
1. Develop LLM prompt and classification schema on 2019-2020 earnings call transcripts (100 transcripts randomly sampled from distinct stocks and sectors).
2. Validate classification accuracy against a human-labeled holdout set of 50 transcripts from 2021 — measure precision and recall of SQUEEZE_SETUP classification.
3. Run full signal on 2022-2023 earnings seasons with locked LLM prompt and classification schema — no parameter tuning.
4. Final out-of-sample: 2024 earnings seasons, with zero changes to any part of the pipeline.
5. Regime test: split samples by VIX regime, by bull/bear market (S&P 500 above/below 200-day moving average), and by earnings season quarter (Q1-Q4) to verify the signal is not concentrated in a single favorable environment.

SELF-ASSESSED CONFIDENCE: Medium
BIGGEST WEAKNESS: The FINRA daily short volume data is the weakest link. It includes substantial market-maker shorting for liquidity provision and options hedging that is NOT directional short positioning — it represents someone filling a customer buy order by shorting temporarily and covering within seconds. The top-quintile filter and borrow-fee overlay mitigate this, but the core data source is inherently noisy. If market-maker short volume spikes around earnings for liquidity reasons (higher volume = more market-making = more short volume mechanically), the signal would fire on many stocks with no genuine short positioning, producing noisy, unprofitable trades. A cleaner data source (actual short interest with daily frequency, like the data that prime brokers sell to institutions) would solve this, but that data is not retail-accessible. The second weakness is earnings call timing: by the time the transcript is published, the stock may have already moved significantly in the after-hours or pre-market session, compressing the remaining mechanical covering potential. The strategy may need to be executed via after-hours trading, which has wider spreads and lower liquidity for retail.

## HYPOTHESIS 3

HYPOTHESIS NAME: ETF Creation Flow and Basket Constituent Liquidity Mismatch
SOURCE AGENT: Microstructure Mechanic
MECHANISM: Authorized participants (APs) that create and redeem ETF shares must buy or sell the underlying basket of stocks. The daily change in ETF shares outstanding — published each evening by ETF issuers — reveals the net dollar flow into or out of the ETF that day. When a major ETF experiences sustained net creation (shares outstanding growing day-over-day for N consecutive days, each day's growth exceeding 1.5 standard deviations above the trailing-90-day average daily change), the APs must purchase the underlying basket constituents. For the smallest, least-liquid stocks in the basket (bottom decile by market capitalization within the ETF's holdings), the AP buying pressure represents a meaningful fraction of the stock's normal daily trading volume — but this buying is not based on any stock-specific fundamental signal. It is purely mechanical portfolio completion. Once the creation flow normalizes (typically within 3-7 trading days), this artificial demand evaporates, and the stock experiences a partial reversal as the mechanical bid disappears. The effect is most pronounced for ETFs with high assets under management, concentrated in stocks where the ETF's incremental demand (implied dollar flow / number of constituents in that size bucket) exceeds 15% of the stock's average daily dollar volume. An LLM filters out stocks where a coincident positive fundamental catalyst (earnings beat, analyst upgrade, sector rotation narrative) could alternatively explain the price movement — isolating the genuinely mechanical flow-driven price moves from coincidentally positive fundamental moves.

LLM ADVANTAGE: Three LLM-specific contributions. First, ETF holdings are disclosed in heterogeneous formats — some issuers publish CSV files, others PDF factsheets, others HTML tables with non-standard column names and formatting. The LLM extracts and standardizes the holdings data (ticker, weight, shares held) across multiple ETF issuers whose formats differ, enabling automated daily processing that would otherwise require manual reformatting or brittle per-issuer parsers. Second, after computing the implied dollar flow into each constituent stock, the LLM reads the stock's recent news, SEC filings, and price/volume context to classify whether any fundamental catalyst exists that could independently explain a positive price move — this is the filtering step that reduces false positives. A stock rising because the company just pre-announced strong earnings is not an ETF-flow trade; the LLM distinguishes correlation from causation by reading the qualitative context. Third, the LLM reads ETF issuer announcements about index methodology changes, rebalancing schedules, and creation/redemption rule modifications (all published in natural language on issuer websites) to identify regime changes that would alter the flow-to-constituent mechanism — for example, when an ETF switches to sampling replication instead of full replication, the mechanical link between shares-outstanding changes and constituent buying breaks.

WHY UNDERWEIGHTED: (1) ETF flow data is public but noisy — most daily shares-outstanding changes are normal trading noise, not sustained creation/redemption flows. Identifying the signal requires multi-day persistence filtering and volatility normalization, which most market participants don't compute systematically. (2) The mapping from ETF shares outstanding to per-constituent dollar flow requires (shares outstanding change * ETF price * constituent weight) for each of potentially hundreds of constituents — computationally straightforward but tedious, and the heterogeneous holdings disclosure formats create a data-wrangling barrier that an LLM eliminates. (3) The effect is capacity-constrained — each individual trade is small (the implied flow into any single small constituent is typically $50K-$500K), making it unattractive to institutional traders but well-suited to retail scale. (4) The strategy requires monitoring dozens of ETFs and hundreds of constituents daily — a task that was prohibitively labor-intensive before LLM automation of the data extraction and filtering pipeline.

HOLDING PERIOD: 1-5 days. Enter at the close on the day the 3-day sustained creation signal fires. Exit after 5 trading days or when the ETF's daily shares-outstanding change drops below 0.5 standard deviations of its average (indicating flow normalization), whichever comes first.
DATA REQUIREMENT:
- ETF daily shares outstanding (published by each ETF issuer on their website, free; e.g., iShares publishes "Shares Outstanding" daily on each fund page under "Key Facts"; State Street SPDR ETFs publish similar data; Vanguard updates daily. Aggregate sources like ETF.com and ETF Database also publish this daily, free with registration).
- ETF holdings with constituent weights (issuer websites update daily; iShares publishes full holdings CSV; SPDR publishes Excel downloads; Vanguard publishes CSV. ETF Database aggregates with a free tier covering most major ETFs. All free).
- ETF price and AUM data (Yahoo Finance, free).
- Constituent stock price, volume, and market cap (Yahoo Finance, free).
- Constituent stock news/filings (SEC EDGAR for 8-K filings, Yahoo Finance news).
- ETF index methodology documents and creation/redemption mechanics descriptions (issuer websites, free PDFs).
- Historical data: 2019-2024.

PROCESSING APPROACH:
1. LLM reads and standardizes the daily ETF holdings file from each ETF issuer's website. The LLM extracts: ticker, name, weight (%), shares held, market value held — from whatever format the issuer publishes (CSV, PDF table, or HTML). Output: standardized tabular data per ETF per day. This is an extraction/standardization task that exploits the LLM's ability to parse heterogeneous structured data embedded in unstructured containers.
2. Deterministic flow calculation: For each ETF, compute daily shares-outstanding change (SO_t - SO_{t-1}). Multiply by ETF closing price to get daily net dollar flow. Compute the trailing 90-day mean and standard deviation of daily dollar flow. Flag ETFs where daily dollar flow exceeds 1.5 sigma for each of the last 3 consecutive trading days (sustained creation signal).
3. For flagged ETFs, compute implied flow per constituent: (ETF daily dollar flow * constituent weight). Flag constituent stocks where (a) the stock is in the bottom decile of market cap within that ETF's holdings, and (b) the implied dollar flow into the stock exceeds 15% of the stock's trailing 20-day average daily dollar volume. These are the stocks where ETF mechanical demand is economically significant relative to normal trading.
4. LLM reads the most recent news and 8-K filings for each flagged constituent stock and classifies whether a fundamental catalyst is present: FUNDAMENTAL_CATALYST (earnings release, guidance change, material contract announcement, M&A, analyst initiation/upgrade with specific fundamental rationale, FDA/regulatory decision), TECHNICAL_CATALYST (ETF rebalancing announcement, index inclusion change — this is related to the signal and does NOT invalidate it), or NO_CATALYST. Stocks with FUNDAMENTAL_CATALYST are excluded; stocks with TECHNICAL_CATALYST or NO_CATALYST pass through.
5. LLM also reads the ETF issuer's index methodology and any recent creation/redemption rule change announcements to verify the creation mechanism is operating normally (full physical replication with no sampling overlay). If the ETF is sampling or has recently modified creation rules, the signal is suppressed for that ETF until the new methodology is confirmed.
6. Signal: Enter long on all qualifying stocks at the close on signal day. Exit after 5 trading days or when the parent ETF's daily shares outstanding change drops below 0.5 sigma (whichever comes first). Maximum position size per stock: 3% of the stock's trailing 20-day average daily dollar volume.

FALSIFIABLE PREDICTION: If this edge is real, the 5-day forward return of qualifying constituent stocks (net of the return of a size-and-sector-matched control stock from the same ETF's holdings that did NOT cross the 15%-of-ADV threshold) should be positive at a rate exceeding 58% hit rate, with mean excess return > 1.0% over 5 days. Concentration of effects: stocks in the bottom 5% (not just bottom 10%) of ETF constituent market cap should show stronger excess returns than those in the 5-10% bucket, since the liquidity mismatch is more acute. If fake, (a) the 5-day excess return distribution should center on zero with a hit rate of 47-53%, and (b) there should be no gradient in excess returns as we move from the smallest to the largest ETF constituents — the liquidity-mismatch mechanism is the causal engine, so if returns are uniform across size buckets, the mechanism is falsified regardless of the aggregate return.

MINIMUM EFFECT SIZE: Mean 5-day excess return of at least 0.80% per qualifying trade, after estimated costs (commission = 0bps; bid-ask spread = 10-20bps for small-cap ETF constituents with moderate liquidity; total one-way cost ~10-20bps). At 0.80% per 5-day hold, with ~60-100 qualifying trades per year (across all major US equity ETFs), capacity-constrained position sizing of 3% of ADV, and compounding, annualized alpha should exceed 6% net of costs. Below 0.80% per trade, the signal is too weak to overcome the variance of individual stock returns — the noise from stock-specific factors (which the LLM catalyst filter attempts to remove, but cannot perfectly eliminate) dominates the signal.

OUT-OF-SAMPLE PLAN:
1. Develop LLM holdings-extraction prompts and news-catalyst classification prompts on 2019-2020 data (ETF holdings files and news articles from that period).
2. Validate holdings extraction accuracy on 50 randomly selected ETF-days from 2021: compare LLM-extracted weights to manually verified weights; target > 99% ticker identification accuracy and > 95% weight accuracy (within 5bps of reported weight).
3. Run full signal on 2022-2023 data with locked prompts — no parameter changes.
4. Final pure out-of-sample: 2024 data.
5. Regime tests: split by (a) ETF type — equity-only ETFs vs. multi-asset ETFs (multi-asset creation/redemption uses different mechanics and should show weaker or absent signal), (b) bull vs. bear market, (c) high vs. low VIX, (d) ETF size — top 20 ETFs by AUM vs. ETFs ranked 21-100 (larger ETFs may have more sophisticated APs that front-run or smooth the flow, reducing the signal).
6. Key robustness check: randomize the signal by shuffling the ETF flow data across dates and ETFs (permutation test). The true signal should be destroyed by randomization; if permuted flows still produce excess returns, the signal is capturing a different effect (e.g., small-cap momentum) rather than the hypothesized ETF flow mechanism.

SELF-ASSESSED CONFIDENCE: Medium
BIGGEST WEAKNESS: The critical assumption is that the ETF issuer's daily shares-outstanding update reflects same-day creation/redemption activity. In practice, ETF shares outstanding are reported on T+1 or even T+2 basis — the creation basket was purchased the prior day, and the price impact has already occurred by the time the data is available to us. If the data lag is T+1 and the price impact is intraday (APs execute the basket purchase during the same session the creation order is submitted), then by the time we observe the shares-outstanding change and place our trade, the price has already absorbed the flow. The strategy would be buying AFTER the mechanical demand has been satisfied — a recipe for entering at inflated prices and then experiencing the post-flow reversal. The edge requires that either (a) the AP executes the basket over multiple sessions, creating a multi-day flow that we can partially capture, or (b) the small-constituent effect is delayed relative to large-constituent buying because APs prioritize liquid names first, leaving the illiquid tail for later execution. Both assumptions need empirical verification and may not hold. Second weakness: ETF creation/redemption is often done in-kind (AP delivers the basket of stocks to the ETF issuer rather than cash), which means no open-market buying occurred — the shares outstanding change reflects an in-kind transfer, not a market purchase. The signal implicitly assumes cash creations dominate, but the prevalence of in-kind vs. cash creation varies by ETF and is not always disclosed in real time.
