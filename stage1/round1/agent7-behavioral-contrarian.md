# Agent 7: Behavioral Contrarian — Round 1 Hypotheses

**Core Belief:** The pain trade — what the market is most desperate to avoid — is often the one that works. LLMs can identify WHEN consensus is fragile by measuring the QUALITY of agreement, not just its existence, across multiple information channels simultaneously.

---

## HYPOTHESIS 1: Earnings Sentiment Argument Monoculture

**SOURCE AGENT:** Behavioral Contrarian

**MECHANISM:** When pre-earnings sentiment across analyst revisions, media articles, and retail options flow is uniformly bullish (or bearish) AND the underlying REASONS given for that sentiment show low semantic diversity (everyone cites the same 1-2 catalysts using near-identical language), the stock is more likely to have a post-earnings move opposite the consensus direction within 1-5 days. The causal chain: low argument diversity means the consensus is an information cascade, not independent verification. When every bull cites "AI pipeline" as the only reason, there is no second buyer behind the first. The trade is crowded, the thesis is brittle, and any earnings nuance that complicates the monoculture narrative triggers a violent repositioning. The market prices consensus DIRECTION but not consensus FRAGILITY.

**LLM ADVANTAGE:** Traditional NLP and quant models measure sentiment DIRECTION (positive/negative) and MAGNITUDE (how positive). An LLM measures sentiment HOMOGENEITY — whether 15 analysts are independently arriving at the same conclusion via different reasoning paths (robust consensus) or whether they are all repeating the same 1-2 arguments in semantically overlapping language (fragile consensus). This requires (a) extracting specific causal claims from unstructured analyst text, (b) embedding those claims, and (c) measuring pairwise semantic similarity. A keyword-overlap approach cannot distinguish "strong pipeline because of Product X launch" from "strong pipeline because of China recovery" — both mention "strong pipeline" but for different reasons. The LLM can.

**WHY UNDERWEIGHTED:** The market uses analyst ratings dispersion (standard deviation of price targets, % Buy vs. Hold) as a consensus measure. Nobody measures WHY analysts agree. Argument diversity requires deep reading of report text and semantic comparison — it falls through the gap between quant screens (which only see numbers) and discretionary analysis (which reads reports but cannot systematically compare across dozens of stocks). It is a genuinely unexamined dimension of sell-side research.

**HOLDING PERIOD:** 1-5 days (earnings event window — enter day before earnings, exit within 5 trading days post-announcement)

**DATA REQUIREMENT:**
- Yahoo Finance (analyst ratings, price target revisions, earnings dates)
- MarketBeat free tier (analyst revision summaries with commentary snippets)
- FINVIZ (news headlines and summaries, free)
- Seeking Alpha free tier (earnings preview articles, delayed)
- CBOE delayed options data / Barchart Unusual Options Activity free tier (retail put/call skew as supplementary sentiment input)
- All free or freemium, retail-accessible.

**PROCESSING APPROACH:** Two-stage LLM pipeline. Stage 1 (aggregation): Collect all analyst report snippets, news articles, and earnings previews mentioning the stock in the 14 calendar days before earnings. Classify each source as bullish, bearish, or neutral. If >75% of sources share the same directional view, the stock enters Stage 2. Stage 2 (diversity measurement): Extract the specific bullish/bearish causal claim from each source (e.g., "margin expansion from lower input costs," "revenue acceleration from new product cycle"). Embed each claim as a vector using a sentence-transformer model. Compute all-pairs cosine similarity across claims within the consensus direction. Compute two metrics: (a) mean pairwise similarity, (b) number of distinct claim clusters (HDBSCAN on embeddings). Low cluster count + high mean similarity = monoculture signal. Combine with a minimum magnitude requirement (e.g., >2% expected move per options market, to ensure there is meaningful money at stake). The trade is a contrarian position (fade the consensus) executed via long puts for bullish monoculture, long calls for bearish monoculture, entered one day before earnings.

**FALSIFIABLE PREDICTION:** If the mechanism is real, stocks in the top quintile of pre-earnings sentiment uniformity (>80% directional agreement) AND bottom quintile of argument diversity (highest mean pairwise similarity) should see a post-earnings move opposite to consensus direction in at least 55% of cases, with the average magnitude of the opposite move exceeding 1.5x the at-the-money straddle cost. If the mechanism is fake, argument diversity provides no incremental predictive power over sentiment direction alone — a logistic regression predicting "move opposite consensus" using both features would assign zero or negative weight to the diversity term after controlling for sentiment uniformity.

**MINIMUM EFFECT SIZE:** 2% excess return over the cost of the options straddle on the opposing side, on a 1-day post-earnings holding period, net of estimated option spread costs (20bps entry + exit). For a stock with a $100 share price and a $7 ATM straddle, this means the opposing directional option must return at least $9 ($2 excess above the $7 breakeven cost).

**OUT-OF-SAMPLE PLAN:** Train the argument extraction prompts and similarity threshold on 2019-2022 earnings seasons (16 quarters). Validate predictive power on 2023-2024 earnings seasons (8 quarters, unseen). Cross-validate by sector (Tech vs. Healthcare vs. Financials) to ensure no single industry drives the result. Also test robustness to minimum-analyst-count thresholds (does the signal weaken when only 5-10 analysts cover the stock vs. 20+?).

**SELF-ASSESSED CONFIDENCE:** Medium. The behavioral logic (information cascades create fragile consensus) is well-established in the academic literature (Bikhchandani et al., Hirshleifer). The novel contribution is measuring cascade severity via semantic argument similarity rather than rating agreement. The key risk is data quality: free analyst report snippets may omit the detailed reasoning needed for meaningful diversity measurement.

**BIGGEST WEAKNESS:** Free-tier analyst report snippets from MarketBeat and Yahoo Finance may provide only rating changes and one-sentence summaries ("raised PT to $150 on growth outlook"), not the detailed causal reasoning the diversity measurement requires. If the extracted "arguments" are too short and generic, the similarity metric will be noisy and the signal will degrade. A validation prerequisite is confirming that at least 40-50% of pre-earnings analyst commentary from free sources contains distinct, parseable causal claims of at least 15-20 words.

## HYPOTHESIS 2: Analyst Initiation Clustering and Bandwagon Classification

**SOURCE AGENT:** Behavioral Contrarian

**MECHANISM:** When three or more sell-side analysts initiate coverage on a stock within a 30-day window with overwhelmingly positive ratings (Buy/Overweight), and the LLM classifies the majority of those initiation reports as "bandwagon" rather than "substantive," the stock tends to underperform its sector over the subsequent 1-4 weeks. The causal mechanism: sell-side initiations are marketing events for the analyst's platform. Analysts initiate on stocks that are already performing well — they are not discovering undervalued names, they are attaching their brand to winners. A clustering of initiations signals that the stock has reached peak "discovery" — every desk that wanted to publish on it has done so. The incremental marginal buyer who would be convinced by a new Buy rating no longer exists. When the initiations lack novel, specific, quantified thesis elements (i.e., they are bandwagon initiations), this confirms the signal: the analysts are not adding new information, they are retroactively justifying an existing price move with generic growth language.

**LLM ADVANTAGE:** A traditional quant screen can count initiations and track rating distributions. It cannot distinguish between a substantive initiation ("We model $4.20 EPS in FY2025 based on a bottom-up analysis of their three new distribution contracts, each worth approximately $50M in annual revenue — our channel checks confirm volume ramp in Q2") and a bandwagon initiation ("The company is a leader in a growing market with strong execution — we see 20% upside as multiple expansion continues."). An LLM can read the initiation commentary and classify whether the thesis contains (a) specific, quantified forward-looking estimates, (b) novel information not already in consensus, and (c) verifiable catalyst timelines. This quality filter transforms a noisy count-based signal into a high-precision contrarian indicator.

**WHY UNDERWEIGHTED:** The market broadly understands that analyst ratings are positively biased. However, the systematic tracking of initiation CLUSTERING as a specific exhaustion signal, combined with automated thesis-quality classification, is not a known strategy. Most quant funds track analyst revisions (changes to existing coverage) because they are higher frequency. Initiations are lower frequency but potentially higher signal-to-noise because they are explicit marketing decisions by the sell-side platform, not incremental estimate updates.

**HOLDING PERIOD:** 1-4 weeks (enter after the third initiation in 30 days, hold for up to 4 weeks or until the stock underperforms its sector by the minimum effect size)

**DATA REQUIREMENT:**
- MarketBeat free tier (analyst initiation alerts with commentary excerpts)
- TipRanks free tier (analyst track records, initiation dates)
- Yahoo Finance (price data, sector ETF data for relative performance)
- FINVIZ (news confirming initiation events and providing additional context)
- All free/freemium, retail-accessible.

**PROCESSING APPROACH:** Two-stage detection. Stage 1 (quantitative screen): Flag any stock where (a) 3+ analysts initiate coverage within a rolling 30-day window, (b) all initiations are Buy/Overweight, and (c) the stock is up at least 15% over the prior 60 trading days (confirms the "already discovered" condition). Stage 2 (LLM classification): For each flagged stock, collect all initiation commentary text from the triggering window. The LLM classifies each initiation report into one of three categories: (i) Substantive — contains specific, quantified forward-looking catalysts with verifiable timeline, (ii) Bandwagon — relies on backward-looking strength, generic sector tailwinds, or vague multiple-expansion arguments, (iii) Mixed — contains some specifics but leans on consensus narratives. Signal fires when >50% of initiations are classified as Bandwagon AND zero initiations are classified as Substantive. The trade is a short (or long put) position relative to sector, sized at 2-3% of portfolio for a $50K-$250K account.

**FALSIFIABLE PREDICTION:** If the mechanism is real, stocks flagged by the combined screen (3+ clustered Buy initiations + majority bandwagon + no substantive) should underperform their sector ETF by at least 3% over the subsequent 4 weeks, with underperformance observed in at least 60% of flagged instances. If the mechanism is fake, the bandwagon-vs-substantive classification adds no predictive power beyond (a) simple 4-week momentum reversal and (b) the raw initiation count. Specifically, a long-short portfolio that shorts bandwagon-clustered stocks and goes long the sector should produce a Sharpe ratio indistinguishable from zero after controlling for momentum and size factors.

**MINIMUM EFFECT SIZE:** 3% excess underperformance relative to the sector ETF over 4 weeks, after estimated costs (10bps borrow cost for hard-to-borrow names, 10bps entry, 10bps exit). Net alpha target: 2.7% over 4 weeks. For a $100K account with a 2% position size ($2,000), this produces $54 in expected profit per trade — modest but meaningful when the signal fires on 15-25 stocks per year.

**OUT-OF-SAMPLE PLAN:** Train the bandwagon-vs-substantive classification prompt/classifier on initiation texts from 2019-2021. Test predictive power for subsequent relative returns on initiations from 2022-2024. Cross-validation dimensions: (a) market cap terciles (small/mid/large), expecting stronger effect in small-mid caps where analyst coverage is sparser and initiations are more impactful events, (b) sector neutrality, (c) robustness to expanding the 30-day window to 45 and 60 days.

**SELF-ASSESSED CONFIDENCE:** Medium-High. The behavioral foundation is solid: analyst initiations as marketing events is well-documented in academic finance (e.g., initiation-related price pops and subsequent drift). The clustering-means-exhaustion logic extends this in a direction that is both intuitive and untested. The LLM quality classification is the novel contribution, and the distinction between "we have a new model with specific numbers" and "this stock is hot" is a classification task that LLMs handle well.

**BIGGEST WEAKNESS:** The low base rate of initiation clustering. How many stocks actually receive 3+ initiations within 30 days? If the universe is only 20-30 stocks per year, the strategy may be too sparse to matter for a retail trader checking once per day. A preliminary base-rate check on 2023 data is essential: count how many US-listed equities with market cap >$500M experienced 3+ initiations in any 30-day window. If the answer is <15, the hypothesis is economically irrelevant. Additionally, the signal is short-biased (fading bullish initiations). In a strong bull market, a short bias may generate negative carry even if the relative underperformance prediction is correct — the stock may still rise, just less than the sector.

## HYPOTHESIS 3: Retail Options Flow Exhaustion with Narrative Context Filter

**SOURCE AGENT:** Behavioral Contrarian

**MECHANISM:** When retail options flow (identified by small-lot, odd-lot order characteristics) shows extreme directional imbalance — >80% of opening premium in one direction (all calls or all puts) — AND the stock has already moved >10% in that same direction over the prior 10 trading days, AND the accompanying social/news narrative is LLM-classified as "FOMO-driven" rather than "catalyst-driven," the stock tends to reverse by at least 2% in the OPPOSITE direction over the next 1-5 trading days. The causal mechanism: retail traders chase momentum with leveraged options late in the move. They are the marginal, least-informed, most-emotional participants. When they arrive in size with no new information catalyst (just "this stock is going up!"), their buying exhausts the last natural demand. The options market makers who sold them those calls/puts now delta-hedge in the OPPOSITE direction as the stock stalls, accelerating the reversal. The narrative filter is essential: extreme retail options flow that arrives alongside a genuine new catalyst (FDA approval, earnings beat, merger announcement) is NOT a fade signal — it is rational repositioning. Extreme flow without a specific catalyst is the exhaustion signal.

**LLM ADVANTAGE:** A purely quantitative screen can identify the options flow imbalance and the prior price move. Traditional quant models can compute put/call ratios, premium imbalances, and order-size distributions. What they cannot do is read the narrative environment and distinguish between "this flow is arriving because the company just announced a breakthrough drug approval" (do NOT fade) and "this flow is arriving because the stock is up 15% in two weeks and Reddit is full of rocket emojis" (FADE). An LLM can ingest the prior week's news headlines, social media posts, and SEC filings, then classify the narrative context into FOMO-driven (emotional, backward-looking, lacking specific near-term catalysts) vs. Catalyst-driven (specific event, quantified impact, forward-looking timeline). This narrative filter eliminates the false positives that make naive options-flow fading unprofitable.

**WHY UNDERWEIGHTED:** Retail options flow is widely followed — Barchart, Unusual Whales, FlowAlgo, and CheddarFlow all have large user bases. However, the dominant use case is COPYING the flow ("smart money" narrative) or using a naive put/call ratio to fade it (which has high false positives). The systematic combination of flow extremes + price exhaustion + narrative context filtering is not a productized strategy. It requires the LLM's ability to read unstructured text and make a contextual classification decision that a simple ratio or screener cannot replicate.

**HOLDING PERIOD:** 1-5 days (enter on the day the combined signal fires, exit when the reversal reaches the minimum effect size or after 5 days, whichever comes first)

**DATA REQUIREMENT:**
- Barchart Unusual Options Activity free tier (option flow with premium, sentiment, trade size)
- Unusual Whales free tier (options flow with order-size classification)
- CBOE delayed options data (free)
- Yahoo Finance (price data, 10-day historical returns)
- StockTwits free API tier / Twitter/X API free tier (social sentiment and narrative content)
- FINVIZ news (headlines and summaries for catalyst identification)
- SEC EDGAR (8-K filings for material event confirmation in the prior 7 days)
- All free or freemium, retail-accessible.

**PROCESSING APPROACH:** Three-stage sequential filter. Stage 1 (quantitative exhaustion screen): Scan the top 500 most-active option names daily. Flag stocks where (a) opening premium imbalance >80% in one direction (calls or puts) on the current trading day, (b) the stock's price has moved >10% in that same direction over the prior 10 trading days, and (c) the options flow is from small-lot/odd-lot orders (signature of retail). Stage 2 (LLM narrative classification): For flagged stocks, collect all news headlines, social media posts (StockTwits trending, Reddit mentions via free API), and SEC 8-K filings from the prior 7 calendar days. The LLM reads this corpus and classifies the narrative environment as either "FOMO-driven" or "Catalyst-driven." FOMO-driven criteria: (i) no material 8-K filed in the prior 7 days, (ii) social media posts are >60% emotional/cheerleading rather than analytical, (iii) news headlines reference the price move itself rather than a fundamental event ("Stock X Surges 20% This Week" vs. "Stock X Wins $500M Government Contract"). Stage 3 (signal execution): If AND ONLY IF Stage 2 returns FOMO-driven, the contrarian signal fires. Enter a position fading the retail flow direction (if retail is buying calls, buy puts; if retail is buying puts, buy calls). Use near-the-money options with 7-14 days to expiration to capture the expected reversal while managing theta decay.

**FALSIFIABLE PREDICTION:** If the mechanism is real, stocks flagged by the combined three-stage screen (quant extreme + FOMO narrative) should reverse by at least 2% in the opposite direction within 5 trading days in >55% of instances, with an average reversal magnitude exceeding 2.5%. Crucially, the narrative filter (Stage 2) must provide INCREMENTAL predictive power: the reversal rate for stocks flagged by Stage 1 + Stage 2 should be significantly higher than the reversal rate for stocks flagged by Stage 1 alone. If the mechanism is fake, adding the FOMO-vs-catalyst narrative classification does not improve the reversal prediction accuracy above and beyond a simple quantitative mean-reversion screen — i.e., the precision of the fade signal with and without the narrative filter is statistically indistinguishable at p<0.05.

**MINIMUM EFFECT SIZE:** 2% reversal in the underlying stock price over 5 trading days, net of options spread costs (estimated 1% round-trip for near-the-money options with 7-14 DTE and ~$0.30 bid-ask spreads). The options position must return at least 3x the premium paid. For a $100K account risking 1% per trade ($1,000), a successful fade returning 100% on the options position produces $1,000 profit. The signal needs a win rate >50% and an asymmetric payoff (winners > losers) to be profitable net of costs.

**OUT-OF-SAMPLE PLAN:** Train the FOMO-vs-catalyst narrative classification prompt and thresholds on 2021-2022 data. Test the full three-stage screen on 2023-2024 data. Cross-validation dimensions: (a) meme stock vs. non-meme stock (expect stronger reversal in meme names where retail participation is highest), (b) market regime (bull vs. bear market — does the signal work in both?), (c) day-of-week effects (is Monday FOMO different from Friday FOMO?), and (d) options expiration week effects (does the signal strengthen or weaken when expiration is near?).

**SELF-ASSESSED CONFIDENCE:** Medium. The behavioral mechanism (retail as late-arriving, emotional, momentum-chasing participants) is well-supported by the literature on retail trading behavior (Barber & Odean, etc.). The novel contribution is the narrative context filter — recognizing that not all retail option flow extremes are created equal. The key uncertainty is whether free data sources provide timely enough options flow data (many free sources have 15-20 minute delays) for a 1-5 day holding period to be actionable.

**BIGGEST WEAKNESS:** Options flow data from free sources is significantly delayed (15-20 minutes for CBOE, potentially end-of-day for Barchart free tier). If the signal requires same-day execution based on same-day flow data, the delay may render the entry stale — the reversal may have already begun by the time the retail trader can act. Mitigating this requires testing whether the signal works when entered the FOLLOWING morning based on the prior day's flow data (T+1 entry). If the reversal happens too quickly (intraday), the signal is not actionable for a once-per-day trader. Additionally, the "retail" classification of options flow (small lots) is imperfect — some institutional algorithms split large orders into odd-lot sizes to hide their footprint, creating false positives in the retail identification. This contaminates the signal with institutional flow that should NOT be faded.
